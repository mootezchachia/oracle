1. **Advance rate math** — 1-2km/month x 3.5 months = 3.5-7km. Druzhkivka is ~20km away. The math doesn't work without a 3x+ breakthrough multiplier, which has occurred zero times in the sustained Donetsk front since 2023.

4. **OSINT transparency** — Any force concentration for a breakthrough attempt would be visible 2-3 weeks in advance via satellite imagery. No such concentration has been detected. The market would reprice before the breakthrough begins.

## Risk Factors
- **Ukrainian defensive collapse** — The primary risk. If a critical Ukrainian unit breaks (ammunition exhaustion, manpower depletion), a cascading collapse could accelerate Russian advance 3-5x. Historical comp: Bakhmut fall led to rapid advances toward Chasiv Yar. Probability: ~8-10%.
- **Russian operational surprise** — A massed armored thrust concentrated on a narrow front could achieve a breakthrough. Russia has not demonstrated this capability since Kherson 2022, but retains the theoretical capacity. Would require force concentration visible on OSINT 2-3 weeks prior.
- **Western aid disruption** — If the Iran conflict causes a genuine diversion of military materiel (not just attention) from Ukraine, defensive capacity degrades. This is the strongest bull case for Yes but is currently not materializing — EU supply chains are independent of US Iran operations.
- **Nuclear/WMD escalation** — A Russian tactical nuclear use or chemical weapon deployment could cause a rapid Ukrainian withdrawal. Extremely low probability but non-zero.
- **Low liquidity risk** — At only $98K volume, this market is thin. Large orders can move price significantly, creating misleading signals.
